Executive Summary

What: Saturday Scouts is a nature-based program for ages 3-6, teaching outdoor skills, building immunity, and developing confidence through gardening, yoga, fire cooking, and nature exploration.

Format: Morning session (10am-12pm), afternoon session (1-3pm), or full day (10am-3pm with lunch). Drop-in weekly‚Äîno term commitment required. Attend one Saturday to try it out, or buy packages for regular attendance.

Capacity: Maximum 20 children per session (scaling to 80 in future)

Foundation: Based on UK Squirrels Scouts program (ages 4-5) with proven results: 78% increased confidence, 82% better teamwork, 87% more willing to try new things

Target Market: Phuket expat families whose children attend traditional schools during the week but need the "nature dimension" they're missing elsewhere‚Äîoutdoor skills, immunity building, and character development that sports and academics don't provide.

Key Advantage: No term commitment required! Parents can "try one Saturday" to experience Bamboo Valley without risk. Once they see the benefits, they buy packages (e.g., "10 sessions + 2 free") for regular attendance. This removes the biggest barrier to enrollment.

Sample Session Structure

Morning Session (10am-12pm)

10:00-10:15 - Opening Ceremony "Gather Palms"

Kids arrive, find their lodge, circle formation under palm trees. Saturday Scouts chant/song. Announce today's badge focus.

10:15-10:30 - Warm-Up Game

Nature-themed active game. Examples: "Monkey in the Middle," "Gecko Tag," palm tree races.

10:30-11:15 - Main Badge Activity

Structured learning + hands-on doing. Examples: Bug hunting with magnifying glasses, building stick teepees, planting in the garden.

11:15-11:30 - Snack & Social Time

Healthy snacks (fruit, crackers), lodge bonding, water break.

11:30-11:50 - Free Exploration or Lodge Project

Child-led activities: mud kitchen, climbing, exploring. OR collaborative lodge project.

11:50-12:00 - Closing Circle

Share discoveries, preview next week, Saturday Scouts goodbye ritual, badge stamps.

Afternoon Session (1pm-3pm)

1:00-1:15 - Re-Gathering & Calm Start

Yoga or stretching under palms, breathing exercises, transition from lunch energy.

1:15-1:30 - Movement Game

Active but not intense (post-lunch). Balance games, nature yoga poses.

1:30-2:15 - Second Badge Activity

Different focus than morning, often more creative/calm. Examples: Story time under palms, harvesting herbs, natural art projects, nature music/sound exploration.

2:15-2:30 - Lodge Project Time

Ongoing projects: Building fairy garden, creating lodge flag, nature journal.

2:30-2:50 - Free Play in Nature

Kids choose their adventure: climbing, digging, exploring, creating.

2:50-3:00 - Closing Ceremony

Circle up, share proudest moment, badge progress check, goodbye ritual.

Full Day Additions (12pm-1pm)

  • 12:00-12:15 - Lunch preparation (kids help set up, wash hands, lodge tables)
  • 12:15-12:45 - Lunch together (family-style serving, social learning)
  • 12:45-1:00 - Quiet/rest time (story time under palms, calm transition)
Fire Cooking Element: Full-day participants experience real campfire cooking (teacher-supervised). Kids learn fire safety, help prepare food, and cook vegetables/simple items over open flame. This is a core Scouts tradition adapted safely for ages 3-6.

How Badge Earning Works

  • Activity-Based, Not Time-Based: Badges are earned by completing specific activities well, not by attending a certain number of weeks. A child who comes once and masters an activity gets that stamp immediately.
  • Stamp System: Each Saturday offers activities tied to different badges. When a child successfully completes an activity (teacher observes skill mastery), they get a stamp in their Scout Book.
  • Earning the Badge: Collect [TBD: 4-6?] stamps for a specific badge type = earn that cloth patch. Example: 5 different "Outdoor Explorer" activities = Outdoor Explorer badge earned.
  • Works for Drop-Ins: A child attending irregularly can still earn badges‚Äîit just takes longer. Regular attendees earn badges faster.
  • Physical Badge: Cloth patch presented when earned, kids sew/iron onto uniform shirt
  • Celebration: Badge earning ceremony during closing circle when milestones are reached

Research & International Inspiration

UK Squirrels Scouts (Ages 4-5)

Launched in 2021 specifically for this age group. Measurable parent-reported results after participation:

  • 78% noticed their child's confidence increased
  • 82% said they had better teamwork skills
  • 87% said they were more likely to try new things
  • 74% more parent involvement compared to other programs

German Waldkindergarten (Forest Kindergarten)

The original model since 1968. Over 1,500 programs across Germany operating 100% outdoors (8:30am-2:30pm, five days a week). Key principle: No commercial toys‚Äîchildren create play with natural objects, building imagination and collaboration.

Swiss Alpine Kindergartens

2003 University of Fribourg study: Forest kindergartners performed equally well on fine motor skills and significantly better on gross motor skills and creativity compared to traditional kindergartens. Switzerland delays academics until age 7, allowing full focus on outdoor exploration.

Norwegian Friluftsbarnehage (Outdoor Life Kindergarten)

5-10% of Norwegian preschoolers attend nature preschools. Cultural philosophy "friluftsliv" (open-air living) means outdoor time isn't alternative education‚Äîit's normal childhood. Famous saying: "There's no bad weather, only bad clothes!"

Singapore Forest Schools

First in Asia to gain UK Forest School accreditation (Dulwich College Singapore). Shows forest school principles work in tropical, urban Asian contexts. Multiple programs thriving in densely populated city-state.

Finnish Immunity Research

University of Helsinki study: Children who played on forest floor (natural soil with living plants) for just 28 days showed measurably stronger immune systems‚Äîmore diverse skin and gut bacteria, increased regulatory T cells, higher anti-inflammatory proteins.
